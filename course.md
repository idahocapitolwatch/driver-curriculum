## 1. Welcome & Orientation

### 1.1 Course Purpose Statement

This course prepares new drivers in Idaho to operate a motor vehicle safely, responsibly, and in full compliance with Idaho traffic laws. Through a structured progression from vehicle basics to complex driving environments, learners develop the knowledge, judgment, and decision-making skills necessary to become safe, lifelong drivers.

### 1.2 Course Narrative

DRV101 is the classroom component of Idaho's approved driver education program. It fulfills the 30-hour classroom instruction requirement of the Idaho Graduated Driver Licensing (GDL) program. This course works in tandem with behind-the-wheel instruction (6 hours driving, 6 hours observation) and supervised practice driving to develop competent, safety-conscious drivers.

### 1.3 Instructor Introduction

The course instructor holds Idaho State Department of Education driver education certification and meets all requirements of the Idaho Driver Education Program Manual. Preferred communication methods and office hours are posted in the course learning management system.

## 2. Course-Level Learning Outcomes

| ID | Course Outcome |
|----|---------------|
| CO-1 | Given knowledge of vehicle systems and controls, demonstrate pre-drive, in-drive, and post-drive procedures that meet Idaho Program Manual benchmarks |
| CO-2 | Given Idaho traffic laws and traffic control devices, apply right-of-way rules and legal requirements in varied intersection and roadway scenarios |
| CO-3 | Given fundamental vehicle control tasks, execute steering, braking, turning, parking, lane change, and backing maneuvers safely |
| CO-4 | Given rural, urban, and highway driving environments, analyze hazards and adapt driving strategies to each |
| CO-5 | Given scenarios involving impairment, distraction, aggression, and emotional states, evaluate risks and apply mitigation strategies |
| CO-6 | Given adverse weather, reduced visibility, or emergency conditions, implement safe driving and response strategies |

## 5. Policies & Expectations

### 5.1 Grading Policy

| Component | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Module Stop-and-Check Quizzes (formative) | 20% |
| Module Activities & Discussions | 15% |
| Unit Capstone Assessments (5 units) | 35% |
| Course Final Assessment | 20% |
| Participation & Engagement | 10% |
| **Total** | **100%** |

**Grading Scale:** A = 90–100%, B = 80–89%, C = 70–79%, D = 60–69%, F = below 60%. A minimum of 70% (C) is required to receive course completion credit toward Idaho GDL requirements.

### 5.2 Late Submission Policy

Late work is accepted up to 3 days past the deadline with a 10% deduction per day. Work submitted more than 3 days late will not be accepted without prior instructor approval or documented extenuating circumstances.

### 5.3 Academic Integrity Policy

All work must be the student's own. Collaboration is permitted on designated discussion and group activities only. Use of AI tools is not permitted on assessments unless explicitly stated. Plagiarism or cheating will result in a zero on the assignment and potential removal from the course.

### 5.4 Communication Expectations

- **Instructor email response:** Within 24 hours on business days
- **Discussion post feedback:** Within 48 hours
- **Graded assignment feedback:** Within 5 business days
- **Preferred channel:** Course LMS messaging

### 5.5 Technology Requirements

- Computer, tablet, or smartphone with current web browser
- Reliable internet connection
- Ability to view video content and participate in online discussions
- PDF viewer for supplementary materials

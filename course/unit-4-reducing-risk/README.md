# Unit 4: Reducing Risk

## Idaho Content Standards

- 1.2 Understanding the Nature of Driving
- 5.2 Time and Space Management Systems and Strategies
- 6.1 The Effects of Gravity and Energy of Motion on a Vehicle
- 9.1 Effects of Emotions on Driving
- 9.2 Disabilities and Driving
- 9.3 Alcohol and Drugs
- 9.4 Alcohol/Drug Involved Crashes and Idaho Law
- 9.5 Drowsy Driving
- 9.6 Aggressive Driving
- 9.7 Distracted Driving

## Unit Objectives

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

1. Explain the four types of distractions - mental, physical, auditory, and visual
2. Describe ways to eliminate distractions from driving
3. Identify aggressive driving behaviors
4. Discuss strategies to reduce aggressive driving behaviors
5. Discuss the effects of drugs and alcohol on a driver's ability to safely operate a vehicle
6. Discuss the effects of emotions, drowsiness and fatigue, and temporary or permanent disabilities on a driver's ability to safely operate a vehicle
7. Discover connections between classroom learning and behind-the-wheel application

## Key Content Topics

### How Alcohol and Drugs Affect the Body
- Blood Alcohol Concentration (BAC) and its meaning
- Factors affecting BAC (weight, gender, food, rate of consumption)
- Alcohol content in different drinks (beer, wine, liquor)
- How drugs (prescription, OTC, illegal) affect driving ability
- Multiplying effects when combining alcohol and drugs
- Individual differences in alcohol/drug response

### Effects on Driving Ability
- **Perception:** Reduced ability to judge distances and speed
- **Vision:** Blurred vision, reduced peripheral vision, poor night vision
- **Reaction Time:** Slower response to hazards
- **Risk-Taking:** Increased willingness to take dangerous risks
- **Coordination:** Reduced fine motor control
- **Judgment:** Impaired decision-making
- Progressive impairment at different BAC levels

### Idaho Impaired Driving Laws
- Legal BAC limits (0.08 adult, 0.02 under 21)
- Zero tolerance for underage drinking and driving
- Implied consent law
- Penalties for DUI (fines, license suspension, jail time, community service)
- Enhanced penalties for repeat offenders
- Ignition interlock requirements
- Drug-related DUI laws

### Teen Impaired Driving Statistics
- Idaho teen DUI statistics
- National teen impaired driving data
- Crash and fatality rates involving impaired teen drivers
- Economic and social costs

### Impact on Families and Communities
- Personal stories and consequences
- Financial impact (legal costs, medical bills, insurance)
- Emotional and psychological effects
- Community impact of impaired driving crashes

### Intervention Strategies
- Recognizing impairment in others
- Taking keys away
- Offering alternative transportation
- Designated driver planning
- Calling a parent, rideshare, or taxi
- Peer pressure resistance
- Being a responsible bystander

## Key Content Topics

### Effects of Emotions on Driving
- Common emotions that affect driving (anger, sadness, excitement, anxiety, stress)
- How emotions impair judgment and increase risk
- Emotional driving behaviors (speeding, tailgating, risk-taking)
- Recognizing when emotions are affecting your driving

### Managing Emotions While Driving
- Self-awareness techniques
- Calming strategies (deep breathing, pulling over)
- Postponing driving when highly emotional
- Avoiding triggers
- Perspective-taking and patience

### Drowsy Driving
- Physical symptoms of fatigue (heavy eyes, yawning, drifting)
- Mental symptoms (difficulty concentrating, missing exits)
- Effects on reaction time, judgment, and attention
- Most dangerous times for drowsy driving
- Teen sleep patterns and driving risk

### Fatigue Prevention Strategies
- Get adequate sleep (7-9 hours for teens)
- Avoid driving during peak drowsiness hours
- Take breaks every 2 hours on long trips
- Share driving responsibilities
- Recognize warning signs early
- Pull over and rest if drowsy
- Caffeine as a temporary aid (not a solution)
- Window down and radio up are NOT effective strategies

### Disabilities and Driving
- **Temporary disabilities:** broken limbs, medication effects, illness, eye injuries
- **Permanent disabilities:** vision impairment, hearing loss, mobility limitations
- Impact on the driving task
- When to avoid driving due to temporary conditions

### Compensating for Disabilities
- Vehicle modifications (hand controls, spinner knobs, pedal extensions)
- Adaptive equipment and technology
- Mirror additions for limited mobility
- Hearing aids and visual alerts for hearing impaired
- Regular vision and medical checks
- Knowing personal limitations
- Legal requirements for medical reporting

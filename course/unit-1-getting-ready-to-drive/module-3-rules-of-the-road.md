## Lesson Objectives

1. **Describe the components of the Highway Transportation System**
   - Correlations: 8.1.1, 8.1.2, 8.1.3, 8.1.4

2. **Explain how traffic laws control traffic**
   - Correlations: 3.1.1, 3.1.2, 3.1.3, 3.1.4, 3.1.5

3. **Define "right of way"**
   - Correlations: 3.2.1-3.2.8, 5.3.1-5.3.6

4. **Recognize and understand traffic signs, signals, and roadway markings**
   - Correlations: 3.1.1, 3.1.2, 3.1.3, 3.1.4, 3.1.5

## Key Content Topics

### Highway Transportation System (HTS)
- People, vehicles, and roadways
- Purpose and function of the HTS
- Personal responsibility within the HTS
- Impact of driver attitudes, choices, and behaviors

### Traffic Control Devices
- Regulatory signs (stop, yield, speed limit, no passing)
- Warning signs (curves, intersections, construction)
- Guide signs (route markers, destination signs)
- Traffic signals (red, yellow, green; arrows; flashing)
- Signal/sign combinations

### Roadway Markings
- Yellow lines (center, no passing, shared turn lanes)
- White lines (lane boundaries, edge lines, crosswalks)
- Special markings (railroad crossings, school zones)
- Pavement arrows and messages

### Traffic Laws
- Speed limits (posted, residential, school zone, construction)
- Right-of-way rules
- Signaling requirements
- Lane use restrictions
- Idaho-specific traffic laws

### Right of Way
- Definition and legal meaning
- Yielding at intersections (controlled and uncontrolled)
- Yielding when merging
- Emergency vehicles, funerals, school buses, pedestrians
- School zones and construction zones
- Railroad grade crossings
- Roundabouts and shared turn lanes

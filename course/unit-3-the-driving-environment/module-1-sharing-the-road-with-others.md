# Module 1: Sharing The Road With Others

**Unit:** 3 - The Driving Environment
**Course:** DRV101 - Driver Education

## Idaho Standards Alignment

| Standard | Benchmark | Description |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| 8.1.1 | HTS Components | Describe the components of the Highway Transportation System |
| 8.1.2 | Driving Impact | Impact and consequences of personal driving attitudes, choices, and behaviors |
| 8.1.3 | Sharing the Road | Driver responsibilities for sharing the road with motorized and non-motorized users and animals |
| 8.1.4 | Traffic Stops | Procedures for traffic stops by a police officer |
| 8.1.5 | Predicting Behavior | How to predict and anticipate the behaviors of other road users |

## Lesson Objectives

1. **Describe the impact and consequences of personal driving attitudes, choices, and behaviors on themselves and others**
   - Correlations: 8.1.1, 8.1.2, 8.1.3, 8.1.4

2. **Explain driver responsibilities for sharing the road with a variety of motorized and non-motorized roadway users**
   - Correlations: 8.1.1, 8.1.2, 8.1.3, 8.1.4

3. **Describe procedures for traffic stops by a police officer**
   - Correlations: 8.1.1, 8.1.2, 8.1.3, 8.1.4

## Key Content Topics

### Highway Transportation System Users
- Passenger vehicles
- Commercial trucks and buses
- Motorcycles and mopeds
- Bicyclists
- Pedestrians
- Emergency vehicles
- Farm equipment and slow-moving vehicles
- Animals (domestic and wildlife)

### Sharing the Road Responsibly
- Maintaining safe distances from different vehicle types
- Understanding blind spots of large vehicles
- Yielding to pedestrians at crosswalks
- Passing bicyclists safely (3-foot rule)
- Responding to emergency vehicles
- Awareness of vulnerable road users

### Personal Driving Attitudes
- How attitudes affect driving behavior
- Consequences of poor choices on self and others
- Personal responsibility in the HTS
- Building a safety-conscious mindset

### Traffic Stops by Law Enforcement
- Pulling over safely (right side of road)
- Turning off engine
- Keeping hands visible
- Providing documents when requested
- Understanding your rights and responsibilities
- Remaining calm and cooperative

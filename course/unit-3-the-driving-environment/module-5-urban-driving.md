## Key Content Topics

### Urban Driving Characteristics
- Higher traffic density
- More intersections and traffic signals
- Pedestrians and cyclists
- Parked vehicles and door zones
- One-way streets
- Multiple lane roads
- Construction zones

### Urban Hazards
- Congestion and stop-and-go traffic
- Limited visibility at intersections
- Double-parked vehicles
- Pedestrians crossing mid-block
- Delivery vehicles blocking lanes
- Aggressive drivers in traffic

### Urban Signs, Signals, and Road Conditions
- Complex intersection signals
- Lane-use signs and markings
- Parking regulations
- One-way street indicators
- Construction zone signage
- Route planning and alternate routes

### Limited Access Highways (Freeways)
- Characteristics: high speed, controlled access, divided roadway
- Advantages: higher speed limits, fewer conflicts, no intersections
- Disadvantages: higher speed crashes, limited exit options, fatigue
- Speed differential awareness

### Freeway Entering and Exiting
- Acceleration lanes (on-ramps)
- Matching traffic speed before merging
- Gap selection for merging
- Deceleration lanes (off-ramps)
- Proper exit procedures
- Missing an exit (proceed to next exit)

### Freeway Lane Choice and Passing
- Travel lane selection (right lane for slower, left for faster)
- Lane change procedures at higher speeds
- Passing safely on freeways
- Maintaining safe following distance at speed
- Effects of higher speed on vehicle control and stopping distance

### Expressway Interchanges
- Cloverleaf interchanges
- Diamond interchanges
- Trumpet interchanges
- Reading interchange signs and lane markings
- Proper positioning for interchange navigation

### Managing Freeway Congestion
- Recognizing congestion patterns
- Planning alternate routes
- Adjusting speed and following distance
- Patience and safety in heavy traffic

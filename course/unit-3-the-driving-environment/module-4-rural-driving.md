## Key Content Topics

### Rural Driving Characteristics
- Higher speed limits
- Two-lane roads with limited passing opportunities
- Limited lighting and signage
- Longer distances between services
- Unpaved or poorly maintained road surfaces
- Limited cell service for emergencies

### Rural Hazards
- Sharp curves and hills with limited visibility
- Narrow bridges and roads
- Loose gravel and uneven surfaces
- Farm equipment and slow-moving vehicles
- Wildlife and livestock on roadways
- Limited shoulders

### Rural Signs, Signals, and Markings
- Rural speed limit signs
- Curve warning signs
- Bridge and road width signs
- Slow-moving vehicle emblems
- Railroad crossing signs and signals
- No-passing zone markings

### Idaho's Open Range Law
- Definition of open range areas
- Driver responsibility in open range areas
- Liability for hitting livestock
- Recognizing open range indicators
- Responding safely to animals on the road

### Railroad Grade Crossings
- Types of railroad crossings (controlled and uncontrolled)
- Warning signs and signals
- Proper procedures for approaching crossings
- When to stop and when to proceed
- Vehicles required to stop at all crossings
- Penalties for crossing violations

### Slow-Moving Vehicles
- Identifying slow-moving vehicle emblems (orange triangle)
- Safe following and passing procedures
- Farm equipment awareness
- Sharing rural roads safely

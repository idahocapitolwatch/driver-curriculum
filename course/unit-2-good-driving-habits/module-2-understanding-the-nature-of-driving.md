## Lesson Objectives

1. **Explain how natural laws affect your driving**
   - Correlations: 6.1.1-6.1.18

2. **Explain how traction and balance are affected by your driving**
   - Correlations: 6.1.1-6.1.18

3. **Describe proper speed and lane positions for approach, apex, and exit on hills and curves**
   - Correlations: 6.2.1-6.2.6

4. **Describe the factors that affect stopping distance**
   - Correlations: 6.1.1-6.1.18

## Key Content Topics

### Gravity and Energy of Motion
- Definition of gravity and kinetic energy
- Relationship between speed and energy (doubling speed quadruples energy)
- Forces of impact in a crash
- How gravity affects vehicles on hills

### Friction and Traction
- Definition of traction and friction
- Tire condition and air pressure effects
- Road surface conditions and traction
- Centrifugal force in curves
- Weight transfer during acceleration, braking, and turning

### Vehicle Balance and Control
- How aggressive driving affects balance
- Load changes: side-to-side, front-to-rear
- Effects of vehicle modifications
- Differences between FWD, RWD, and AWD in traction loss
- Managing traction loss and skids

### Braking and Stopping Distance
- Perception distance
- Reaction distance
- Braking distance
- Total stopping distance
- Factors that increase stopping distance
- Anti-lock braking systems (ABS) - function and technique
- Stability and traction control systems

### Negotiating Hills and Curves
- Line of sight and path of travel on hills/curves
- Proper approach speed and positioning
- Ascending and descending hills safely
- Stopping and starting on hills
- Approach, apex, and exit technique for curves
- Maintaining traction on hills and curves

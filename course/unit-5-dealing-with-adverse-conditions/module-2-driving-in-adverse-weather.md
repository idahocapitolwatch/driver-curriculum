1. **Describe adverse weather driving**

## Key Content Topics

### Rain and Wet Roads
- Hydroplaning: causes, prevention, and recovery
- Reduced traction on wet surfaces
- First rain after dry spell (oil on road surface)
- Spray from other vehicles reducing visibility
- Proper speed reduction
- Increased following distance
- Gentle braking and steering inputs
- Using headlights in rain

### Snow and Ice
- Black ice awareness and identification
- Reduced traction and longer stopping distances
- Proper tire condition and chains (when required)
- Gentle acceleration, braking, and steering
- Bridge and overpass freezing
- Snow-packed and icy road techniques
- Winter driving preparation (emergency kit)
- Idaho winter driving conditions

### Fog
- Reducing speed significantly
- Using low-beam headlights (not high beams)
- Using fog lights when available
- Increasing following distance substantially
- Listening for traffic when visibility is near zero
- Using roadway markings as guides
- Pulling off the road completely if conditions are too dangerous

### Wind
- Effects of crosswinds on vehicle control
- High-profile vehicles and wind vulnerability
- Adjusting steering to compensate
- Reducing speed in high winds
- Wind gusts near overpasses, gaps in terrain, and large vehicles
- Dust storms and reduced visibility

### Temperature Extremes
- Hot weather effects on tires and engine
- Cold weather starting and warming
- Battery and fluid considerations
- Road surface changes with temperature

### General Adverse Weather Strategies
- Check weather before driving
- Postpone trips when conditions are severe
- Allow extra travel time
- Reduce speed to match conditions
- Increase following distance
- Use appropriate lights
- Avoid sudden inputs (steering, braking, acceleration)
- Emergency supply kit for winter driving
- Know when NOT to drive

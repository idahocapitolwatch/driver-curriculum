## Key Content Topics

### Limited Visibility Conditions
- Nighttime driving
- Dawn and dusk (transitional light)
- Fog, mist, and haze
- Rain, snow, and dust
- Smoke from fires
- Sun glare

### Sources and Management of Glare
- Oncoming headlights
- Sun position (sunrise/sunset)
- Wet road surface reflections
- Dashboard reflections on windshield
- Using sun visor effectively
- Clean windshield inside and out
- Night driving glasses (anti-glare)
- Looking to the right edge of the road when facing glare

### Night Driving Strategies
- Reduce speed below daytime levels
- Increase following distance
- Use headlights to see and be seen
- Adjust interior lighting to minimum
- Keep eyes moving and scan more frequently
- Watch for pedestrians and animals
- Allow extra time for eyes to adjust to darkness
- Avoid looking directly at oncoming headlights

### Idaho Headlight Laws and Proper Use
- When headlights are required (30 minutes after sunset to 30 minutes before sunrise)
- Required in reduced visibility conditions
- Low beam vs. high beam usage
- When to dim high beams (500 feet from oncoming, 300 feet when following)
- Daytime running lights
- Parking lights (when to use and not use)
- Headlight maintenance (clean lenses, proper aim)

### Risk Reduction Procedures
- Slow down to match visibility distance
- Ensure headlights and taillights are clean and working
- Keep windshield clean
- Use fog lights when available (and when appropriate)
- Increase following distance
- Avoid sudden braking
- Be prepared for unexpected hazards

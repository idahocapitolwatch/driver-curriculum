## Key Content Topics

### Collision Avoidance Strategies
- Maintaining safe following distance
- Scanning and anticipating hazards
- Having an escape route planned
- Proper speed for conditions
- Minimizing risk through space management
- Idaho crash statistics and common driver errors

### The Three Collisions of a Crash
1. **Vehicle collision:** The vehicle strikes an object or another vehicle
2. **Human collision:** Occupants strike the interior of the vehicle
3. **Internal collision:** Internal organs strike the skeletal structure
- Effects on restrained vs. unrestrained occupants
- Why seatbelts and airbags matter at each collision stage

### Occupant Protection Systems
- **Seatbelts:** Proper wearing, lap and shoulder belt function
- **Airbags:** Locations (front, side, curtain), deployment, proper seating distance
- **Head restraints:** Proper adjustment to prevent whiplash
- **Belt adjusters:** Proper use for smaller occupants
- **Child restraint systems:** Age and size requirements

### What to Do at a Crash Scene
1. Stop immediately (leaving the scene is a crime)
2. Protect the scene (hazard lights, flares/triangles)
3. Call 911 if there are injuries or significant damage
4. Provide aid if qualified (see Good Samaritan Law)
5. Exchange information with other drivers
6. Document the scene (photos, notes)
7. Cooperate with law enforcement
8. Notify insurance company
9. Follow emergency personnel's directions

### Idaho's Good Samaritan Law
- Protection from civil liability when providing emergency aid
- Requirements: act in good faith, not grossly negligent
- Encouragement to help injured persons
- Limitations of the law

### When Law Enforcement Must Be Called
- Any crash with injuries or fatalities
- Crashes with significant property damage (Idaho threshold)
- Hit-and-run situations
- Crashes involving government vehicles
- When drivers cannot agree on fault
- DUI-involved crashes

### Idaho Vehicle Insurance Requirements
- Minimum liability coverage amounts
- Proof of insurance requirements
- Penalties for driving without insurance
- Uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage
- What to do if the other driver is uninsured

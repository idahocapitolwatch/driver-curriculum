## Key Content Topics

### Tire Emergencies
- **Blowout/sudden deflation:** Grip steering firmly, ease off gas, do NOT brake hard, steer straight, gradually slow down, pull off road
- Recognizing signs of tire failure
- Changing a flat tire (procedure and safety)
- Tire maintenance to prevent failures

### Accelerator Problems
- **Stuck accelerator:** Shift to neutral, apply brakes firmly, steer to safety, turn off engine after stopping
- Engine surge response
- Electronic throttle system awareness

### Engine Failure
- Loss of power steering and power brakes
- Steering to the shoulder
- Using remaining brake pressure (pump if needed)
- Hazard lights activation
- Getting safely off the roadway

### Cooling System Failure
- Recognizing overheating (temperature gauge, steam)
- Turning off A/C, turning on heater
- Pulling over safely
- NOT opening radiator cap when hot

### Steering Failure
- Power steering loss (increased effort required)
- Complete steering failure response
- Braking to a stop

### Electrical System and Lighting Failure
- Headlight failure procedures
- Using parking lights as temporary measure
- Pulling over safely

### Brake Failure
- Pump brake pedal rapidly
- Downshift to lower gear
- Apply parking/emergency brake gradually
- Steer toward an uphill or soft surface
- Use friction surfaces as last resort

### Vehicle Fire
- Pull over immediately
- Turn off engine
- Get all occupants away from vehicle
- Do NOT open the hood
- Call 911

### Skid Response
- **Front-wheel skid (understeer):** Ease off gas, do not brake, steer in direction of travel
- **Rear-wheel skid (oversteer):** Ease off gas, steer in direction you want the front to go
- **All-wheel skid (lockup):** Release brake pressure, regain traction, then brake again
- ABS vs. non-ABS braking in skid situations
- Prevention through smooth inputs

### Emergency Evasive Steering
- When to steer vs. brake
- Quick lane change technique
- Avoiding head-on collisions
- Steering around obstacles

### Off-Road Recovery
- Do NOT jerk the wheel back onto the road
- Grip steering firmly
- Ease off gas gradually
- Straddle the edge of pavement
- When speed is reduced, steer gently back onto road
- Check traffic before returning to roadway

## Unit 1: Getting Ready to Drive

**Idaho Standards:** 2.1, 2.2, 2.3, 2.4, 2.5, 3.1, 3.2, 4.1, 4.2, 5.2, 5.3, 8.1

**Unit Objectives:**
- Identify the components of the Highway Transportation System
- Identify vehicle equipment and safety features
- Identify intersection types and apply rules of right of way for each
- Interact with other class participants to develop a culture of collaboration

## Unit 2: Good Driving Habits

**Idaho Standards:** 1.1, 1.2, 4.1, 4.2, 4.3, 4.4, 4.5, 5.1, 5.2, 6.1, 6.2

**Unit Objectives:**
- Explain the Idaho Graduated License requirements
- Explain how to manage the space margin around your vehicle
- Explain the use of a selective search pattern to minimize risk
- Explain how gravity, energy of motion, and other natural laws affect a vehicle
- Explain multiple ways to communicate with other drivers
- Discover connections between classroom learning and behind-the-wheel application

## Unit 3: The Driving Environment

**Idaho Standards:** 3.1, 3.2, 4.2, 4.3, 4.4, 4.5, 5.3, 7.1, 7.2, 7.3, 8.1

**Unit Objectives:**
- Describe ways to safely share the road with various roadway users
- Identify various parking configurations
- Identify safe procedures for lane changes and passing
- Describe characteristics and hazards of rural driving environments
- Describe characteristics and hazards of urban driving environments
- Discuss the safety features and risks involved in freeway driving
- Discover connections between classroom learning and behind-the-wheel application

## Unit 4: Reducing Risk

**Idaho Standards:** 1.2, 5.2, 6.1, 9.1, 9.2, 9.3, 9.4, 9.5, 9.6, 9.7

**Unit Objectives:**
- Explain the four types of distractions - mental, physical, auditory, and visual
- Describe ways to eliminate distractions from driving
- Identify aggressive driving behaviors
- Discuss strategies to reduce aggressive driving behaviors
- Discuss the effects of drugs and alcohol on a driver's ability to safely operate a vehicle
- Discuss the effects of emotions, drowsiness and fatigue, and temporary or permanent disabilities on a driver's ability to safely operate a vehicle
- Discover connections between classroom learning and behind-the-wheel application

## Unit 5: Dealing With Adverse Conditions

**Idaho Standards:** 2.4, 2.5, 7.4, 7.5, 8.2, 8.3

**Unit Objectives:**
- Identify risks associated with and strategies to compensate for reduced visibility driving conditions
- Identify risks associated with and strategies to compensate for driving during adverse weather conditions
- Explain ways to minimize the risk of vehicle failures
- Identify ways to avoid a collision and, if necessary, minimize the damage of a collision
- Explain the requirements for what to do after a collision
- Demonstrate or discuss safe procedures for changing a flat tire
- Discover connections between classroom learning and behind-the-wheel application

## 3. Pre-Work Plan

**Prior Knowledge Activation:**
- In Module 2.3, you learned that vision leads all driving decisions. What happens to your driving when your eyes are off the road for even 3 seconds?
- Think about the last time you saw a driver distracted by a phone. What was dangerous about their behavior?

**Pre-Reading/Viewing:**
- Idaho Driver's Manual, section on Distracted Driving (~6 pages)
- Guiding questions: What four types of distraction does the manual describe? What is Idaho's law on mobile device use while driving?

**Readiness Self-Check:**
1. Can you name four types of driving distractions?
2. How many seconds does it take to read a text at 55 mph, and how far do you travel?
3. Do you know Idaho's law about texting while driving?
4. What is one strategy to reduce passenger distractions?

**Key Terms:**
1. Visual distraction
2. Manual (physical) distraction
3. Cognitive (mental) distraction
4. Auditory distraction
5. Texting while driving
6. Hands-free
7. GDL passenger restrictions
8. Inattention blindness
9. Distracted driving crash

## 5. Activity Plan

### Activity 1: Distraction Classification Exercise

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-4.1.1
**Estimated Time:** 10 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Review 12 distracted driving scenarios.
2. Classify each by distraction type(s) — note that many involve multiple types.
3. For each, write one sentence explaining why this distraction impairs driving.
4. Check your answers.

**Deliverable:** Completed classification chart with explanations

### Activity 2: Distraction Source Mapping

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-4.1.2
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Complete the source-to-type matrix: for each distraction source (phone, GPS, passenger, food, radio, pet, emotional state, billboard), check which distraction types apply.
2. Rank the sources from most dangerous to least dangerous, explaining your ranking.
3. Identify which sources are covered by Idaho law.

**Deliverable:** Completed matrix with ranking and legal notes

### Activity 3: Distracted Driving Pledge and Discussion

**Type:** Interaction
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Learner
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-4.1.3
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** Yes (5% of module grade)

**Instructions:**
1. Write a personal distraction-free driving pledge that includes at least 5 specific commitments (e.g., "I will put my phone in the glove box before starting the car").
2. Post your pledge to the discussion board.
3. Respond to one classmate's pledge — suggest an additional commitment or share why you agree with one of theirs.

**Deliverable:** Pledge post (minimum 100 words) + 1 response (minimum 30 words)

## 6. Assessment Plan

### Assessment: Module 4.1 Stop and Check Quiz

**Type:** Quiz
**MLO(s) Assessed:** MLO-4.1.1, MLO-4.1.2, MLO-4.1.3
**Weight:** 10% of module grade
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Attempts:** 2 allowed

**Description:**
Quiz covering distraction types, sources, statistics, reduction strategies, and Idaho law.

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Distraction Classification (MLO-4.1.1) | Correctly classifies 90%+ of scenarios by type with clear explanations | Classifies 80–89% correctly | Classifies 60–79% correctly | Classifies fewer than 60% correctly |
| Source Identification (MLO-4.1.2) | Explains all assessed sources with correct types and risks | Explains most; minor gap | Explains some; notable confusion | Cannot explain distraction sources |
| Strategy Application (MLO-4.1.3) | Selects and justifies correct strategies for all scenarios with Idaho law cited | Selects correctly for most; minor justification gap | Selects for some; strategies vague | Cannot select appropriate strategies |

**Academic Integrity Notice:**
Complete individually. Notes and manual may be referenced. Do not share answers. AI tool use is not permitted.

## 7. Reflection & Synthesis Plan

**Key Takeaways:**
1. Texting while driving combines all four distraction types — it is one of the most dangerous behaviors a driver can engage in.
2. Even "hands-free" technology creates cognitive distraction — your mind is off the road even if your hands are on the wheel.
3. Idaho law prohibits texting while driving, and GDL restrictions limit passengers for good reason — teen passengers increase crash risk.
4. The best strategy is prevention: put the phone away, set GPS before driving, and commit to distraction-free driving.

**Connection to Next Module:**
Module 4.2 (Aggressive Driving) covers another major risk factor — how anger and impatience impair driving just as dangerously as distraction.

**Self-Reflection Prompt:**
1. Be honest: what is your biggest potential distraction as a driver? What will you do about it?
2. How does distracted driving connect to the stopping distance concepts from Module 2.2?
3. If a passenger asks you to look at something on their phone while you are driving, what will you say?

## 3. Pre-Work Plan

**Prior Knowledge Activation:**
- In Module 4.2, you learned how emotions impair judgment and driving. Now think: how does alcohol impair those same abilities even more severely?
- What do you already know about Idaho's DUI laws for drivers under 21?

**Pre-Reading/Viewing:**
- Idaho Driver's Manual, section on Alcohol, Drugs, and Driving (~10 pages)
- Guiding questions: What is the legal BAC limit in Idaho? What is "zero tolerance" for underage drivers? What is "implied consent"?

**Readiness Self-Check:**
1. What does BAC stand for?
2. Is one beer, one glass of wine, and one shot of liquor roughly the same amount of alcohol?
3. What is the legal BAC limit for adult drivers in Idaho?
4. What happens if an underage driver is caught with any alcohol in their system?

**Key Terms:**
1. Blood Alcohol Concentration (BAC)
2. Impairment
3. Zero tolerance
4. Implied consent
5. DUI (Driving Under the Influence)
6. Designated driver
7. Standard drink
8. Synergistic effect
9. Ignition interlock

## 5. Activity Plan

### Activity 1: BAC and Impairment Analysis

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-4.3.1
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Using the BAC chart, determine the BAC for 5 different scenarios (varying weight, gender, number of drinks, time).
2. For each BAC level, identify the specific impairments that occur (perception, vision, reaction time, judgment, coordination).
3. Determine whether the person is legally impaired under Idaho law.
4. Check your answers.

**Deliverable:** Completed BAC analysis for 5 scenarios with impairment lists

### Activity 2: Idaho DUI Law Scenarios

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-4.3.2
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Read 6 scenarios involving impaired driving situations.
2. For each, identify: (a) Is the driver legally impaired? (b) What Idaho law applies? (c) What are the specific penalties?
3. Compare to model answers.

**Deliverable:** Written law application for 6 scenarios

### Activity 3: Intervention Strategy Discussion

**Type:** Interaction
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Learner
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-4.3.3
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** Yes (5% of module grade)

**Instructions:**
1. Read the provided scenario: A friend at a party has been drinking and insists they are "fine to drive."
2. In your discussion post, describe at least three specific intervention strategies you would use, explaining why each is effective.
3. Respond to one classmate's post — identify which strategy you think is most effective and why.

**Deliverable:** Discussion post (minimum 100 words) + 1 response (minimum 30 words)

## 6. Assessment Plan

### Assessment: Module 4.3 Stop and Check Quizzes

**Type:** Quiz (2 quizzes)
**MLO(s) Assessed:** MLO-4.3.1, MLO-4.3.2, MLO-4.3.3
**Weight:** 10% of module grade
**Duration:** 20 minutes total
**Attempts:** 2 allowed

**Description:**
Quiz 1 covers alcohol/drug effects and BAC-impairment relationships. Quiz 2 covers Idaho law, penalties, and intervention strategies.

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Substance Effects (MLO-4.3.1) | Explains all impairment categories and BAC relationships correctly | Explains most; minor gap in one category | Explains some; key relationships missing | Cannot explain substance effects on driving |
| Idaho Law Application (MLO-4.3.2) | Applies correct laws and penalties at 90%+ accuracy | Applies at 80–89% accuracy | Applies at 60–79% accuracy | Applies at less than 60% accuracy |
| Intervention Strategies (MLO-4.3.3) | Identifies 3+ safe, effective strategies with clear justification | Identifies 2–3 strategies; justification adequate | Identifies 1–2; justification weak | Cannot identify appropriate strategies |

**Academic Integrity Notice:**
Complete individually. Notes and manual may be referenced. Do not share answers. AI tool use is not permitted.

## 7. Reflection & Synthesis Plan

**Key Takeaways:**
1. Alcohol and drugs impair every ability needed for safe driving: perception, vision, reaction time, judgment, and coordination.
2. BAC rises based on body weight, drink count, rate of consumption, and time — there is no way to "sober up" quickly.
3. Idaho enforces zero tolerance for underage drinking and driving (0.02 BAC) with severe penalties.
4. Intervention is everyone's responsibility — taking keys, calling a ride, or involving a trusted adult can save lives.

**Connection to Next Module:**
Module 4.4 (Other Risky Behaviors) covers additional impairments — emotions, fatigue, and disabilities — that compromise driving safety in ways similar to substance impairment.

**Self-Reflection Prompt:**
1. What specific words would you use to stop a friend from driving impaired?
2. How does learning about BAC and impairment change the way you think about "just one drink"?
3. Why do you think teen impaired driving rates remain high despite education and enforcement?

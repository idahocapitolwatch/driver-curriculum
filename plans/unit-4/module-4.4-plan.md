## 3. Pre-Work Plan

**Prior Knowledge Activation:**
- In Module 4.3, you learned how substances impair driving. Now think: can your own emotional state impair driving just as much, even without any substance?
- Have you ever felt so tired that you couldn't focus on what you were reading? How would that affect driving?

**Pre-Reading/Viewing:**
- Idaho Driver's Manual, sections on Emotions, Fatigue, and Disabilities (~8 pages)
- Guiding questions: How can anger affect your driving decisions? What are the warning signs of drowsy driving?

**Readiness Self-Check:**
1. Can you name two emotions that impair driving?
2. What is the recommended amount of sleep for a teenager?
3. What should you do if you feel drowsy while driving?
4. Can a person with a physical disability legally drive?

**Key Terms:**
1. Emotional impairment
2. Road rage (emotional escalation)
3. Drowsy driving
4. Fatigue
5. Microsleep
6. Circadian rhythm
7. Temporary disability
8. Permanent disability
9. Adaptive equipment

## 5. Activity Plan

### Activity 1: Emotion-Driving Impact Analysis

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-4.4.1
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. For each of 5 emotions (anger, sadness, excitement, anxiety, stress), describe:
   - How it affects driving judgment
   - How it changes driving behavior
   - Two specific management strategies
2. Compare to model answers.

**Deliverable:** Completed emotion-impact chart with management strategies

### Activity 2: Drowsy Driving Warning Signs Checklist

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-4.4.2
**Estimated Time:** 10 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Review the list of 12 potential warning signs.
2. Classify each as a physical symptom or mental symptom of fatigue.
3. For each, select the appropriate prevention strategy from the provided list.
4. Identify the 3 signs that indicate you must stop driving immediately.
5. Check your answers.

**Deliverable:** Completed symptom classification with prevention strategies

### Activity 3: Disability Compensation Discussion

**Type:** Interaction
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Learner
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-4.4.3
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** Yes (5% of module grade)

**Instructions:**
1. Choose either temporary or permanent disabilities.
2. Describe two specific disabilities in that category and explain how each affects the driving task.
3. For each, describe the compensatory actions or adaptive equipment that enables safe driving.
4. Respond to one classmate who chose the other category.

**Deliverable:** Discussion post (minimum 100 words) + 1 response (minimum 30 words)

## 6. Assessment Plan

### Assessment: Module 4.4 Stop and Check Quizzes

**Type:** Quiz (2 quizzes)
**MLO(s) Assessed:** MLO-4.4.1, MLO-4.4.2, MLO-4.4.3
**Weight:** 10% of module grade
**Duration:** 15 minutes total
**Attempts:** 2 allowed

**Description:**
Quiz 1 covers emotions and drowsy driving. Quiz 2 covers disabilities and compensatory actions.

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Emotional Effects (MLO-4.4.1) | Explains all emotions' effects with 2+ strategies each, no errors | Explains most; one strategy gap | Explains some; strategies incomplete | Cannot explain emotional effects on driving |
| Drowsy Driving (MLO-4.4.2) | Identifies all symptoms and matches correct strategies | Identifies most; minor mismatch | Identifies some; notable gaps | Cannot identify drowsy driving signs |
| Disability Compensation (MLO-4.4.3) | Explains effects and compensations for all assessed disabilities | Explains most; minor omission | Explains some; key compensations missing | Cannot explain disability effects or compensations |

**Academic Integrity Notice:**
Complete individually. Notes and manual may be referenced. Do not share answers. AI tool use is not permitted.

## 7. Reflection & Synthesis Plan

**Key Takeaways:**
1. Strong emotions — whether negative (anger, sadness) or positive (excitement) — impair judgment, increase risk-taking, and reduce attention.
2. Drowsy driving is comparable to drunk driving in its effects on reaction time and awareness — the only cure is sleep.
3. Both temporary and permanent disabilities can be accommodated with adaptive equipment and driving modifications — knowing personal limitations is key.
4. The decision to drive or not drive when emotionally upset, fatigued, or physically impaired is one of the most important safety decisions you will make.

**Connection to Next Module:**
Unit 5 begins with Module 5.1 (Reduced Visibility Driving), where you will learn to manage environmental conditions that increase driving risk — building on the risk awareness skills from this entire unit.

**Self-Reflection Prompt:**
1. Have you ever been so tired that you struggled to focus? How would that affect your driving?
2. What is your plan for managing emotions before getting behind the wheel?
3. How does learning about adaptive equipment change your perspective on who can be a safe driver?

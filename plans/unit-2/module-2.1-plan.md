## 3. Pre-Work Plan

**Prior Knowledge Activation:**
- What do you already know about getting a driver's license in Idaho? What steps do you think are involved?
- Why do you think Idaho has a graduated licensing system for teen drivers instead of giving full licenses right away?

**Pre-Reading/Viewing:**
- Idaho Driver's Manual, section on Licensing Requirements (~8 pages)
- Guiding questions: What age can you first get a permit? How long must you hold a permit before getting a restricted license?

**Readiness Self-Check:**
1. Do you know the minimum age for a driver training permit in Idaho?
2. Can you name one restriction placed on new drivers?
3. Do you know what GDL stands for?
4. Can you explain what "supervised driving" means?

**Key Terms:**
1. Graduated Driver Licensing (GDL)
2. Driver training permit (Class D)
3. Supervised instruction period
4. Restricted driver's license
5. Full driver's license
6. Endorsement
7. Suspension
8. Revocation
9. Supervised Driving Guide
10. Behind-the-wheel instruction

## 4. Core Instruction Plan

**Primary Modalities:** Text + Visual (timeline/flowchart) + Interactive

**Content Chunks:**

| Chunk | Topic | Duration | Engagement Prompt |
|-------|-------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Purpose of the Idaho GDL Program | 7 min | "Why do you think crash rates are highest for new drivers in their first year?" |
| 2 | Stage 1: Supervised Instruction Permit | 8 min | "What restrictions make sense at this stage? Would you add any?" |
| 3 | Stage 2: Restricted License | 8 min | "Why are nighttime and passenger restrictions important for new drivers?" |
| 4 | Stage 3: Full License | 5 min | "What responsibilities come with having a full, unrestricted license?" |
| 5 | Course Requirements (30 hr classroom, 6 hr BTW, 6 hr observation) | 7 min | "How does observation time help you become a better driver?" |
| 6 | Maintaining Privileges: Violations, Suspensions, Revocations | 8 min | "What is the difference between a suspension and a revocation?" |
| 7 | Roles: Instructor, Parent, and Student Responsibilities | 7 min | "How can your parent/guardian support your driving practice outside of class?" |

**Worked Examples:**
- GDL Timeline: Visual walkthrough of a 16-year-old going through all three stages with dates, requirements met, and restrictions lifted
- Violation consequence flowchart: Starting from a speeding ticket, showing how points accumulate and when suspension occurs

## 5. Activity Plan

### Activity 1: GDL Stages Timeline Activity

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-2.1.1
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Using the blank GDL timeline template, fill in each stage with: minimum age, required time in stage, restrictions, and what triggers advancement to the next stage.
2. Calculate your personal projected dates for each stage based on your current age.
3. Check your completed timeline against the answer key.

**Deliverable:** Completed GDL timeline with personal projected dates

### Activity 2: License Type Comparison Chart

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-2.1.2, MLO-2.1.3
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Complete the comparison chart for: Driver Training Permit, Restricted License, and Full License.
2. For each, list: eligibility requirements, restrictions, privileges, and consequences of violations.
3. Review your answers against the completed chart.

**Deliverable:** Completed license comparison chart

### Activity 3: Violation Consequences Scenarios

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-2.1.3
**Estimated Time:** 10 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Read 5 scenarios describing teen driver violations (e.g., speeding, driving past curfew, texting).
2. For each, identify the likely consequence under Idaho law.
3. Compare to the model answers.

**Deliverable:** Written consequence identification for each scenario

## 6. Assessment Plan

### Assessment: Module 2.1 Stop and Check Quiz

**Type:** Quiz
**MLO(s) Assessed:** MLO-2.1.1, MLO-2.1.2, MLO-2.1.3
**Weight:** 10% of module grade
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Attempts:** 2 allowed

**Description:**
Selected-response and short-answer quiz on GDL stages, license types, course requirements, and consequences of violations.

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| GDL Stages (MLO-2.1.1) | Explains all three stages accurately with requirements and restrictions | Explains stages with minor inaccuracies | Explains some stages; notable gaps | Cannot explain GDL stages |
| License Classification (MLO-2.1.2) | Classifies all license types with correct requirements and privileges | Classifies most correctly; minor errors | Classifies some; confusion between types | Cannot classify license types |
| Violation Consequences (MLO-2.1.3) | Correctly identifies consequences for 90%+ of scenarios | Correctly identifies 80–89% | Correctly identifies 60–79% | Correctly identifies fewer than 60% |

**Academic Integrity Notice:**
Complete individually. Notes and Idaho Driver's Manual may be referenced. Do not share answers. AI tool use is not permitted.

## 7. Reflection & Synthesis Plan

**Key Takeaways:**
1. Idaho's GDL system is designed to gradually increase driving privileges as experience grows — each stage builds on the previous one.
2. Permit and restricted license restrictions (nighttime driving, passenger limits) exist because these are the highest-risk situations for new drivers.
3. Driving is a privilege, not a right — violations have real consequences including suspension and revocation.

**Connection to Next Module:**
Module 2.2 (Understanding the Nature of Driving) explains the physics that make driving risky — the natural laws that even experienced drivers must respect.

**Self-Reflection Prompt:**
1. Which GDL restriction do you think is most important for keeping new drivers safe?
2. How will you use the Supervised Driving Guide to track and improve your practice?
3. What is one thing you will commit to doing differently now that you understand the consequences of driving violations?

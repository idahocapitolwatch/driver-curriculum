## 3. Pre-Work Plan

**Prior Knowledge Activation:**
- In Module 2.3, you learned about managing visibility. Now think: once you see a hazard, how do you manage the space around your vehicle to avoid it?
- What do you think is the most important "zone" around your car — front, sides, or rear?

**Pre-Reading/Viewing:**
- Idaho Driver's Manual, sections on Space Management and Lane Positioning (~10 pages)
- Guiding questions: What are the five steps of SIPDE? What is the minimum safe following distance?

**Readiness Self-Check:**
1. Can you explain what SIPDE stands for?
2. How many seconds of following distance should you maintain?
3. What are "reference points" in driving?
4. How do you communicate with other drivers besides using turn signals?

**Key Terms:**
1. SIPDE (Search, Identify, Predict, Decide, Execute)
2. Zone control
3. Lane position
4. Reference point
5. Following distance
6. Safe gap
7. Blind area
8. Line of sight
9. Path of travel
10. Tailgating

## 4. Core Instruction Plan

**Primary Modalities:** Text + Visual diagrams + Video

**Content Chunks:**

| Chunk | Topic | Duration | Engagement Prompt |
|-------|-------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | SIPDE System: Search, Identify, Predict | 8 min | "Give an example of a hazard you would Search for, Identify, and Predict at a busy intersection." |
| 2 | SIPDE System: Decide, Execute | 7 min | "You predict a pedestrian will step off the curb. What are your Decide and Execute options?" |
| 3 | Six Zones Around the Vehicle | 8 min | "Which zones are hardest to monitor? How can you keep them clear?" |
| 4 | Lane Positions and Reference Points | 8 min | "When would you use lane position 3 (left of center) vs. position 4 (far right)?" |
| 5 | Following Distance and Safe Gap Selection | 8 min | "Why is 3 seconds the minimum, not the recommended, following distance?" |
| 6 | Communication Techniques | 7 min | "When is it appropriate to use your horn, and when should you avoid it?" |
| 7 | Idaho Crash Statistics and Risk Reduction | 7 min | "Which driver error causes the most crashes in Idaho? How does space management prevent it?" |

**Worked Examples:**
- SIPDE application: Step-by-step walkthrough of approaching a school zone — each SIPDE step narrated with decision rationale
- Lane position selection: Overhead diagram of a narrow road with oncoming bicycle — choosing LP3 vs. LP4 with explanation

## 5. Activity Plan

### Activity 1: SIPDE Step-Through Exercise

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-2.4.1
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. View the 4 driving scenario images provided.
2. For each, write out all five SIPDE steps with specific actions for that scenario.
3. Identify which zones are affected in each scenario.
4. Compare your responses to the model answers.

**Deliverable:** SIPDE step-through for 4 scenarios with zone identification

### Activity 2: Lane Position Scenario Analysis

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-2.4.2, MLO-2.4.3
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Review 6 overhead driving diagrams showing different traffic situations.
2. For each, select the correct lane position (LP1–LP5) and explain your choice.
3. Identify the communication technique you would use in each.
4. Check your answers.

**Deliverable:** Lane position selections with explanations and communication choices

### Activity 3: Consequences of Driving Errors Activity

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-2.4.4
**Estimated Time:** 20 minutes
**Graded:** Yes (10% of module grade)

**Instructions:**
1. Review the Idaho crash statistics data provided.
2. Identify the top 3 driver errors that lead to crashes.
3. For each error, explain how applying SIPDE and space management would reduce that risk.
4. Write a one-paragraph summary connecting crash data to personal driving responsibility.

**Deliverable:** Written crash data analysis (minimum 200 words)

## 6. Assessment Plan

### Assessment: Module 2.4 Stop and Check Quizzes

**Type:** Quiz (3 quizzes)
**MLO(s) Assessed:** MLO-2.4.1, MLO-2.4.2, MLO-2.4.3
**Weight:** 10% of module grade
**Duration:** 20 minutes total
**Attempts:** 2 allowed

**Description:**
Three short quizzes: (1) SIPDE and zones, (2) lane positioning and following distance, (3) communication techniques.

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| SIPDE/Zones (MLO-2.4.1) | Explains all five SIPDE steps and six zones with no errors | Explains most correctly; minor gap | Explains some; notable confusion | Cannot explain SIPDE or zone system |
| Lane Position Selection (MLO-2.4.2) | Selects correct position in 90%+ of scenarios with clear reasoning | Selects correctly in 80–89%; reasoning mostly sound | Selects correctly in 60–79%; reasoning unclear | Cannot reliably select appropriate lane positions |
| Communication (MLO-2.4.3) | Selects correct technique for all assessed scenarios | Selects correctly for most; one error | Selects correctly for some; multiple errors | Cannot match communication techniques to scenarios |

**Academic Integrity Notice:**
Complete individually. Notes and manual may be referenced. Do not share answers. AI tool use is not permitted.

## 7. Reflection & Synthesis Plan

**Key Takeaways:**
1. SIPDE is your decision-making framework — Search, Identify, Predict, Decide, Execute — applied constantly while driving.
2. The six zones around your vehicle must be monitored continuously — maintaining open zones gives you escape options.
3. Lane position and following distance are your primary tools for managing space — both should adjust to conditions.
4. Communication (signals, lights, horn, position) makes your intentions clear to other road users.
5. Most crashes result from preventable driver errors — space management directly addresses the most common ones.

**Connection to Next Module:**
Unit 3 begins with Module 3.1 (Sharing the Road With Others), where you will apply space management principles specifically to interactions with different types of road users.

**Self-Reflection Prompt:**
1. Which step of SIPDE do you think you will find most challenging to practice consistently?
2. How does the Consequences Activity change the way you think about personal driving responsibility?
3. How do visibility management (Module 2.3) and space management (this module) work together?

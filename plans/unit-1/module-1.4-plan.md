## 3. Pre-Work Plan

**Prior Knowledge Activation:**
- In Module 1.3, you learned about traffic signs, signals, and right-of-way definitions. Now think: at a four-way stop with no traffic signals, how do you decide who goes first?
- Have you ever been in a car when an emergency vehicle approached with lights and sirens? What did the driver do?

**Pre-Reading/Viewing:**
- Idaho Driver's Manual, sections on Intersections and Right of Way (~10 pages)
- Guiding questions: What are the rules at an uncontrolled intersection? When must you stop for a school bus?

**Readiness Self-Check:**
1. Can you define "right of way"?
2. Do you know the difference between a controlled and uncontrolled intersection?
3. What should you do when you hear a siren behind you?
4. What does a round yellow sign with an "X" and "RR" mean?

**Key Terms:**
1. Controlled intersection
2. Uncontrolled intersection
3. T-intersection
4. Roundabout
5. Right of way
6. Yield
7. Staggered stop
8. Safety stop position
9. Railroad grade crossing
10. Funeral procession

## 4. Core Instruction Plan

**Primary Modalities:** Text + Visual diagrams + Video

**Content Chunks:**

| Chunk | Topic | Duration | Engagement Prompt |
|-------|-------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Types of Intersections (controlled, uncontrolled, T, multi-lane) | 8 min | "Which intersection type do you think is the most dangerous? Why?" |
| 2 | Roundabouts and Their Navigation | 7 min | "Why are roundabouts considered safer than traditional intersections?" |
| 3 | Visual Search Pattern at Intersections (left-front-right-rear) | 8 min | "Why do we look left first at an intersection?" |
| 4 | Right-of-Way Rules at Four-Way and Two-Way Stops | 8 min | "Two cars arrive at a four-way stop at the same time. Who goes first?" |
| 5 | Right of Way at Uncontrolled Intersections | 7 min | "What is the default rule when there are no signs or signals?" |
| 6 | Yielding to Emergency Vehicles, School Buses, Pedestrians | 8 min | "What is the penalty for passing a stopped school bus?" |
| 7 | Railroad Crossings and Special Zones | 8 min | "Why must certain vehicles stop at every railroad crossing, even when no train is visible?" |

**Worked Examples:**
- Four-way stop scenario: Three cars arrive — narrated walkthrough of who proceeds in what order and why
- Roundabout navigation: Overhead diagram with step-by-step entry, yield, and exit

## 5. Activity Plan

### Activity 1: Intersection Type Identification

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-1.4.1
**Estimated Time:** 10 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Review 10 intersection images/diagrams.
2. Identify the type of each intersection.
3. For each, list the correct approach procedure.
4. Check your answers against the key.

**Deliverable:** Completed identification worksheet

### Activity 2: Right-of-Way Scenario Cards

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-1.4.2, MLO-1.4.3
**Estimated Time:** 20 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Review 8 illustrated right-of-way scenarios (four-way stops, uncontrolled intersections, emergency vehicle encounters, school bus stops, railroad crossings).
2. For each scenario, determine which vehicle proceeds first and explain the rule that applies.
3. Write your answers and compare to the model responses.

**Deliverable:** Written scenario analyses with rule citations

### Activity 3: Intersection Navigation Discussion

**Type:** Interaction
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Learner
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-1.4.1, MLO-1.4.2
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** Yes (5% of module grade)

**Instructions:**
1. The instructor posts a complex intersection scenario with multiple vehicles.
2. In your post, describe who has the right of way and why, referencing specific rules.
3. Respond to one classmate — explain whether you agree or disagree and why.

**Deliverable:** Discussion post (minimum 75 words) + 1 response (minimum 30 words)

## 6. Assessment Plan

### Assessment: Module 1.4 Stop and Check Quizzes

**Type:** Quiz (2 quizzes)
**MLO(s) Assessed:** MLO-1.4.1, MLO-1.4.2, MLO-1.4.3
**Weight:** 10% of module grade
**Duration:** 15 minutes total
**Attempts:** 2 allowed

**Description:**
Quiz 1 covers intersection types and navigation. Quiz 2 covers right-of-way rules including special yielding situations.

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Intersection Identification (MLO-1.4.1) | Identifies all intersection types and procedures correctly | Identifies most types; minor procedure gap | Identifies some types; notable gaps | Cannot reliably identify intersection types |
| Right-of-Way Application (MLO-1.4.2) | Applies yielding rules correctly in 90%+ of scenarios | Applies correctly in 80–89% of scenarios | Applies correctly in 60–79% of scenarios | Applies correctly in fewer than 60% |
| Special Yielding Procedures (MLO-1.4.3) | Explains all special yielding situations with complete steps | Explains most situations; minor omission | Explains some; critical steps missing | Cannot explain special yielding procedures |

**Academic Integrity Notice:**
Complete individually. Notes and Idaho Driver's Manual may be referenced. Do not share answers. AI tool use is not permitted.

## 7. Reflection & Synthesis Plan

**Key Takeaways:**
1. Intersections are where most crashes occur — knowing the type of intersection determines your approach strategy.
2. Right-of-way is not something you "have" — it is something you yield to others. Always be prepared to yield even when you technically have the right of way.
3. Special yielding rules (emergency vehicles, school buses, pedestrians, railroad crossings) are non-negotiable and carry serious penalties if violated.
4. A systematic visual search pattern (left-front-right-rear) at every intersection reduces crash risk significantly.

**Connection to Next Module:**
Unit 2 begins with Module 2.1 (The Licensing Process), which explains the Idaho GDL system that allows you to practice these skills legally on the road.

**Self-Reflection Prompt:**
1. Which right-of-way rule was most surprising to you, and how will you remember it?
2. How does the visual search pattern (left-front-right-rear) apply to what you learned about visibility in Module 1.1?
3. Why is the concept of "yielding" right of way more important than "having" right of way?

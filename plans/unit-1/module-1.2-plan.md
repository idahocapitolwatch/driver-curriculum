## 3. Pre-Work Plan

**Prior Knowledge Activation:**
- In Module 1.1, you learned about steering wheel controls and pedals. Now think: what do you think determines whether you should use one steering technique versus another?
- Have you ever watched someone back up a car? What did they do with their mirrors and body position?

**Pre-Reading/Viewing:**
- Idaho Driver's Manual, section on Basic Vehicle Control (~8 pages)
- Guiding questions: What is the recommended hand position on the steering wheel? What three types of braking does the manual describe?

**Readiness Self-Check:**
1. Can you name the three pedals in a standard vehicle (or two in an automatic)?
2. Do you know which direction to turn the steering wheel to go left?
3. Can you explain what ABS stands for?
4. What do you think "threshold braking" means?

**Key Terms:**
1. Hand-over-hand steering
2. Push-pull steering
3. Controlled braking
4. Threshold braking
5. Trail braking
6. Backing
7. Reference point
8. Acceleration control
9. ABS (Anti-lock Braking System)
10. Gap selection

## 5. Activity Plan

### Activity 1: Steering Technique Matching

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-1.2.1
**Estimated Time:** 10 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Read each of the 8 driving scenarios provided.
2. Select the correct steering technique for each (hand-over-hand, push-pull, or one-hand).
3. Review immediate feedback explaining why each technique is appropriate.

**Deliverable:** Completed matching exercise (auto-scored)

### Activity 2: Braking Scenario Analysis

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-1.2.2
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Review the 6 braking scenarios provided (e.g., "approaching a red light," "deer jumps into road," "gradual curve on wet road").
2. For each scenario, identify the correct braking type and explain your reasoning in 1–2 sentences.
3. Compare your answers to the model responses provided.

**Deliverable:** Written scenario responses

### Activity 3: Backing Procedure Sequencing

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-1.2.3
**Estimated Time:** 10 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Arrange the 10 steps for backing straight into the correct sequence.
2. Then arrange the 10 steps for backing while turning into the correct sequence.
3. Check your answers against the correct sequence.

**Deliverable:** Two completed sequencing exercises

## 6. Assessment Plan

### Assessment: Module 1.2 Stop and Check Quizzes

**Type:** Quiz (3 short quizzes)
**MLO(s) Assessed:** MLO-1.2.1, MLO-1.2.2, MLO-1.2.3
**Weight:** 10% of module grade
**Duration:** 15 minutes total
**Attempts:** 2 allowed (highest score kept)

**Description:**
Three short quizzes covering steering techniques, braking types, and backing/maneuvering procedures. Questions combine selected-response and short-answer formats.

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Steering Technique Selection (MLO-1.2.1) | Correctly matches all steering techniques to appropriate scenarios | Correctly matches most techniques; 1 error | Matches some correctly; 2–3 errors | Cannot reliably match techniques to scenarios |
| Braking Type Differentiation (MLO-1.2.2) | Accurately differentiates all three braking types with correct applications | Differentiates types with minor confusion between two | Identifies one type correctly; confuses the others | Cannot differentiate braking types |
| Backing Procedures (MLO-1.2.3) | Describes all critical steps in correct order for both straight and turning backing | Describes most steps correctly; minor sequence issue | Describes some steps; notable omissions or sequence errors | Cannot describe backing procedures meaningfully |

**Academic Integrity Notice:**
Complete individually. Notes and Idaho Driver's Manual may be referenced. Do not share answers. AI tool use is not permitted.

## 7. Reflection & Synthesis Plan

**Key Takeaways:**
1. Different steering techniques serve different situations — choosing the right one improves control and safety.
2. Understanding the three braking types (controlled, threshold, trail) prepares you for normal and emergency stops.
3. Backing requires slower speed, multiple mirror checks, and direct observation — rushing causes collisions.
4. Entering and leaving the roadway safely requires signaling, gap selection, and speed matching.

**Connection to Next Module:**
Module 1.3 (Rules of the Road) will teach you the traffic laws and signs that govern how and where you use these maneuvers on public roads.

**Self-Reflection Prompt:**
1. Which maneuver do you think will be hardest for you to master during behind-the-wheel training, and why?
2. How does understanding braking distance change the way you think about following other cars?
3. What is one thing you will do differently the first time you sit in the driver's seat, based on what you learned?

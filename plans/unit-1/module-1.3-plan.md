## 3. Pre-Work Plan

**Prior Knowledge Activation:**
- Think about the last time you were a passenger in a car. How many different types of signs did you notice? What colors and shapes do you remember?
- What do you think would happen if there were no traffic laws?

**Pre-Reading/Viewing:**
- Idaho Driver's Manual, sections on Traffic Signs, Signals, and Markings (~12 pages)
- Guiding questions: How many shapes of regulatory signs can you identify? What do yellow road markings mean versus white markings?

**Readiness Self-Check:**
1. Can you name two regulatory signs?
2. What does a solid yellow line on the road mean?
3. What color is a warning sign?
4. What does a flashing red traffic signal mean?

**Key Terms:**
1. Highway Transportation System (HTS)
2. Regulatory sign
3. Warning sign
4. Guide sign
5. Traffic signal
6. Right of way
7. Roadway markings
8. Speed limit
9. Yield
10. Crosswalk

## 4. Core Instruction Plan

**Primary Modalities:** Text + Visual (sign/marking images) + Interactive exercise

**Content Chunks:**

| Chunk | Topic | Duration | Engagement Prompt |
|-------|-------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Highway Transportation System: People, Vehicles, Roadways | 7 min | "Which component of the HTS do you think causes the most crashes — people, vehicles, or roadways?" |
| 2 | Regulatory Signs (stop, yield, speed limit, no passing) | 8 min | "Why are regulatory signs different shapes and colors? How does this help drivers?" |
| 3 | Warning and Guide Signs | 8 min | "What would happen if a driver ignored a curve warning sign on a mountain road?" |
| 4 | Traffic Signals (steady, flashing, arrows) | 8 min | "What is the difference between a steady yellow and a flashing yellow signal?" |
| 5 | Roadway Markings: Yellow Lines | 7 min | "Why are center line markings yellow and lane markings white?" |
| 6 | Roadway Markings: White Lines and Special Markings | 7 min | "Can you cross a solid white line? Under what circumstances?" |
| 7 | Idaho Traffic Laws Overview | 8 min | "Name one Idaho traffic law that you think new drivers break most often." |

**Worked Examples:**
- Annotated intersection photo showing multiple signs, signals, and markings — step-by-step explanation of what each requires the driver to do
- Side-by-side comparison of commonly confused signs (yield vs. merge, no passing vs. passing permitted)

**Supplementary Resources:**

## 5. Activity Plan

### Activity 1: HTS Component Mapping

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-1.3.1
**Estimated Time:** 10 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Review the HTS diagram provided.
2. List three examples for each HTS component (people, vehicles, roadways).
3. For each component, write one sentence explaining how it contributes to safe traffic flow.

**Deliverable:** Completed HTS mapping worksheet

### Activity 2: Sign Classification Activity

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-1.3.2, MLO-1.3.3
**Estimated Time:** 20 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Review 20 traffic sign and roadway marking images.
2. Classify each sign by type (regulatory, warning, guide) or identify the marking meaning.
3. For each, write the required driver response.
4. Check against the answer key.

**Deliverable:** Completed classification chart with driver responses

### Activity 3: Intersection Scenario Discussion (Unit-Level Interaction)

**Type:** Interaction
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Learner
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-1.3.1, MLO-1.3.2, MLO-1.3.3
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** Yes (5% of module grade)

**Instructions:**
1. View the intersection scenario image posted by the instructor.
2. In a discussion post, identify all signs, signals, and markings visible and describe the correct driver response.
3. Respond to one classmate's post — agree, disagree, or add observations they missed.

**Deliverable:** Discussion post (minimum 75 words) + 1 peer response (minimum 30 words)

## 6. Assessment Plan

### Assessment: Module 1.3 Stop and Check Quizzes

**Type:** Quiz (2 quizzes)
**MLO(s) Assessed:** MLO-1.3.1, MLO-1.3.2, MLO-1.3.3
**Weight:** 10% of module grade
**Duration:** 15 minutes total
**Attempts:** 2 allowed

**Description:**
Two short quizzes. Quiz 1 covers the HTS and traffic law concepts. Quiz 2 covers sign/signal/marking identification and required responses.

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| HTS Knowledge (MLO-1.3.1) | Correctly identifies all three components and explains contributions clearly | Identifies components; explanations mostly clear | Identifies two of three; explanations vague | Cannot identify HTS components |
| Sign/Signal Classification (MLO-1.3.2) | Correctly classifies 90%+ of signs/signals with accurate driver responses | Classifies 80–89% correctly | Classifies 60–79% correctly | Classifies fewer than 60% correctly |
| Roadway Marking Interpretation (MLO-1.3.3) | Explains all assessed markings correctly with legal responses | Explains most markings; minor errors | Explains some markings; notable confusion | Cannot explain roadway markings meaningfully |

**Academic Integrity Notice:**
Complete individually. Notes and Idaho Driver's Manual may be referenced. Do not share answers. AI tool use is not permitted.

## 7. Reflection & Synthesis Plan

**Key Takeaways:**
1. The HTS is a system of people, vehicles, and roadways — driver behavior is the most variable and controllable factor.
2. Traffic signs use shape and color consistently — learning the system helps you respond even to unfamiliar signs.
3. Roadway markings communicate lane use rules and passing restrictions — yellow = opposite direction traffic, white = same direction.
4. Idaho traffic laws establish the rules every driver must follow to keep the HTS safe and orderly.

**Connection to Next Module:**
Module 1.4 (Intersections and Right of Way) will apply these rules specifically to intersection navigation — where most crashes occur.

**Self-Reflection Prompt:**
1. Which type of traffic sign did you find most confusing, and what will you do to learn it better?
2. How does understanding the HTS change the way you think about driving as a responsibility?
3. Next time you're a passenger, observe three signs you haven't noticed before. What do they mean?

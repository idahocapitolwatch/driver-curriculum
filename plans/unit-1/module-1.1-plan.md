## 3. Pre-Work Plan

**Prior Knowledge Activation:**
- What do you already know about the controls in a car? Have you ever sat in the driver's seat and looked at the dashboard?
- Think of three things a driver must do before starting a car.

**Pre-Reading/Viewing:**
- Idaho Driver's Manual, Chapter on Vehicle Equipment (assigned section, ~10 pages)
- Guiding questions: What are the three most important dashboard warning lights? What should you check before entering a vehicle?

**Readiness Self-Check:**
1. Can you name at least 3 dashboard gauges?
2. Do you know what a turn signal lever controls?
3. Can you describe what a seatbelt does in a crash?
4. Have you heard of adaptive cruise control or lane departure warning?

**Key Terms:**
1. Dashboard
2. Gauges
3. ADAS (Advanced Driver-Assistance Systems)
4. Blind spot
5. Turn signal
6. Parking brake
7. Headrest
8. BGE mirror method
9. Hazard lights
10. Owner's manual

## 4. Core Instruction Plan

**Primary Modalities:** Text + Video + Visual diagrams

**Content Chunks:**

| Chunk | Topic | Duration | Engagement Prompt |
|-------|-------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Dashboard Gauges and Warning Lights | 8 min | "Which warning light do you think is most critical to respond to immediately? Why?" |
| 2 | Vehicle Control Devices (steering, pedals, gear selector) | 8 min | "In your own words, describe the difference between the accelerator and brake pedal functions." |
| 3 | Safety and Communication Devices (signals, horn, lights, mirrors) | 8 min | "Why do you think mirrors are considered both a safety AND communication device?" |
| 4 | ADAS: Identification and Categories | 8 min | "Which ADAS feature do you think has prevented the most crashes? Explain your reasoning." |
| 5 | ADAS: Benefits, Limitations, and Proper Use | 7 min | "Why is it dangerous to rely entirely on ADAS instead of your own awareness?" |
| 6 | Preparing to Drive: Pre-Entry and Entry Tasks | 8 min | "Create a mental checklist: what would you do from the moment you approach the car to the moment you start the engine?" |
| 7 | Mirror Adjustment and Securing the Vehicle | 7 min | "What is the advantage of the BGE mirror method over traditional mirror positioning?" |

**Worked Examples:**
- Step-by-step walkthrough of a complete pre-drive routine (video demonstration with narration)
- Annotated dashboard photo identifying each gauge and warning light

## 5. Activity Plan

### Activity 1: Dashboard Identification Practice

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-1.1.1
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Review the labeled dashboard diagram provided.
2. Complete the drag-and-drop matching exercise pairing each gauge/warning light to its function.
3. Check your answers using the immediate feedback feature.
4. Retry any items answered incorrectly.

**Deliverable:** Completed matching exercise (auto-scored, unlimited attempts)

### Activity 2: Vehicle Controls and ADAS Sorting

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-1.1.2, MLO-1.1.3
**Estimated Time:** 20 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Given a list of 15 vehicle features, categorize each as: Control Device, Safety Device, Communication Device, or ADAS Feature.
2. For each ADAS feature, write one benefit and one limitation.
3. Submit your completed categorization chart.

**Deliverable:** Completed categorization chart with ADAS annotations

### Activity 3: Meet Your Classmates Discussion

**Type:** Interaction
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Learner
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-1.1.4
**Estimated Time:** 20 minutes
**Graded:** Yes (5% of module grade)

**Instructions:**
1. Introduce yourself to the class (name, why you want to learn to drive).
2. Describe one thing you already knew about vehicles before starting this module and one thing that surprised you.
3. Respond to at least two classmates' posts with a substantive comment or question.

**Deliverable:** Initial post (minimum 75 words) + 2 peer responses (minimum 30 words each)

## 6. Assessment Plan

### Assessment: Module 1.1 Stop and Check Quiz

**Type:** Quiz
**MLO(s) Assessed:** MLO-1.1.1, MLO-1.1.2, MLO-1.1.3, MLO-1.1.4
**Weight:** 10% of module grade
**Duration:** 20 minutes
**Attempts:** 2 allowed (highest score kept)

**Description:**
Selected-response quiz covering dashboard gauges, vehicle controls, ADAS features, and pre-drive procedures. Questions are drawn from the module content and assess recall and comprehension of vehicle systems and preparation steps.

**Instructions:**
1. Read each question carefully before selecting your answer.
2. Answer all questions — there is no penalty for guessing.
3. Submit your quiz when complete.

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Gauge & Warning Identification (MLO-1.1.1) | Correctly identifies 9–10 of 10 gauges/warnings | Correctly identifies 7–8 of 10 | Correctly identifies 5–6 of 10 | Correctly identifies fewer than 5 of 10 |
| Control Device Knowledge (MLO-1.1.2) | Accurately explains all assessed control devices | Accurately explains most control devices with minor gaps | Explains some control devices; notable gaps | Cannot accurately explain most control devices |
| ADAS Understanding (MLO-1.1.3) | Correctly classifies all ADAS features; identifies benefits and limitations for each | Correctly classifies most; benefits/limitations mostly accurate | Classifies some correctly; benefits/limitations incomplete | Unable to classify most ADAS features |
| Pre-Drive Procedures (MLO-1.1.4) | Lists all steps in correct sequence with no omissions | Lists most steps in correct sequence; minor omission | Lists some steps; sequence errors or omissions | Cannot list pre-drive steps in meaningful sequence |

**Academic Integrity Notice:**
This quiz is to be completed individually. You may reference your notes and the Idaho Driver's Manual. Do not share questions or answers with classmates. Use of AI tools to generate answers is not permitted.

## 7. Reflection & Synthesis Plan

**Key Takeaways:**
1. Dashboard gauges and warning lights communicate critical vehicle status — recognizing them quickly can prevent breakdowns and crashes.
2. Vehicle control devices (steering, pedals, signals) and safety devices (seatbelts, mirrors, hazard lights) work together as an integrated system.
3. ADAS technologies assist but do not replace the driver — understanding their limitations is essential.
4. A consistent pre-drive routine ensures the vehicle is safe to operate before every trip.

**Connection to Next Module:**
Module 1.2 (Basic Maneuvers) builds directly on this foundation — you will learn how to use the controls you identified here to steer, accelerate, brake, and back the vehicle.

**Self-Reflection Prompt:**
1. What was the most important thing you learned about your vehicle in this module?
2. Which ADAS feature do you think is most important for new drivers, and why?
3. How will a consistent pre-drive routine help you become a safer driver?

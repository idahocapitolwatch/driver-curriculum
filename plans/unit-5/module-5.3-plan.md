## 3. Pre-Work Plan

**Prior Knowledge Activation:**
- In Module 2.2, you learned about traction and vehicle balance. How do traction loss and skids relate to what you will learn about emergency responses?
- In Module 5.2, you learned about adverse weather. Many emergencies happen because of weather conditions. What is the connection?

**Pre-Reading/Viewing:**
- Idaho Driver's Manual, section on Emergencies (~8 pages)
- Guiding questions: What should you do if your tire blows out? How do you recover from a rear-wheel skid?

**Readiness Self-Check:**
1. If your brakes fail, what should you do first?
2. What does "steer into the skid" mean?
3. If your right wheels drop off the pavement, should you immediately steer back onto the road?
4. What should you do if your car catches fire?

**Key Terms:**
1. Blowout
2. Brake failure
3. Engine failure
4. Stuck accelerator
5. Understeer (front-wheel skid)
6. Oversteer (rear-wheel skid)
7. ABS
8. Off-road recovery
9. Emergency evasive steering

## 4. Core Instruction Plan

**Primary Modalities:** Text + Visual diagrams + Video

**Content Chunks:**

| Chunk | Topic | Duration | Engagement Prompt |
|-------|-------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Tire Blowout: Recognition and Response | 8 min | "Why is the instinct to brake hard during a blowout the worst thing you can do?" |
| 2 | Brake Failure Response | 8 min | "Why should you pump the brake pedal first before trying other methods?" |
| 3 | Engine and Accelerator Emergencies | 7 min | "What happens to power steering and power brakes when the engine dies?" |
| 4 | Electrical Failure, Steering Failure, and Vehicle Fire | 8 min | "In a vehicle fire, why should you NOT open the hood?" |
| 5 | Front-Wheel Skid (Understeer) Recovery | 8 min | "In a front-wheel skid, why do you ease off the gas instead of braking?" |
| 6 | Rear-Wheel Skid (Oversteer) and All-Wheel Skid Recovery | 8 min | "How does ABS change the recovery technique for an all-wheel skid?" |
| 7 | Emergency Evasive Steering and Off-Road Recovery | 8 min | "Why must you NOT jerk the wheel when your tires drop off the pavement?" |

**Worked Examples:**
- Tire blowout recovery: Step-by-step procedure with annotated diagram showing grip steering → ease off gas → steer straight → gradually slow → pull off
- Off-road recovery: Overhead diagram showing gradual deceleration, straddling pavement edge, and gentle return to roadway

## 5. Activity Plan

### Activity 1: Vehicle Failure Response Matching

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-5.3.1
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Match each vehicle failure type to its correct response procedure.
2. Then arrange the steps of each response in correct sequence.
3. Identify the most critical "do NOT" instruction for each failure type.
4. Check your answers.

**Deliverable:** Completed matching and sequencing exercises

### Activity 2: Skid Response Analysis

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-5.3.2
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Review 6 skid scenarios with diagrams showing vehicle position and direction of skid.
2. For each, identify: (a) the skid type (front, rear, or all-wheel), (b) the cause, (c) the correct recovery steps.
3. Explain how ABS changes the response for applicable scenarios.
4. Compare to model answers.

**Deliverable:** Written skid analyses with recovery steps

### Activity 3: Emergency Response Discussion

**Type:** Interaction
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Learner
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-5.3.1, MLO-5.3.3
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** Yes (5% of module grade)

**Instructions:**
1. Choose the emergency scenario you think is most likely for a new Idaho driver (blowout, brake failure, skid, off-road, engine failure).
2. Post the complete step-by-step response and explain why this scenario is most likely.
3. Respond to one classmate who chose a different scenario — what would you add?

**Deliverable:** Discussion post (minimum 100 words) + 1 response (minimum 30 words)

## 7. Reflection & Synthesis Plan

**Key Takeaways:**
1. Vehicle emergencies require immediate, practiced responses — knowing the correct procedure before an emergency occurs is critical.
2. The correct response to most emergencies (blowout, skid, off-road) involves easing off the gas and NOT braking hard — contrary to instinct.
3. Skid types require different recovery techniques — front-wheel, rear-wheel, and all-wheel skids each have distinct responses.
4. Off-road recovery must be gradual — jerking the wheel to return to the road will cause a rollover.
5. Prevention through vehicle maintenance, appropriate speed, and smooth inputs is always better than emergency response.

**Connection to Next Module:**
Module 5.4 (Collisions) covers what happens when a collision cannot be avoided — crash physics, post-crash responsibilities, and Idaho law.

**Self-Reflection Prompt:**
1. Which emergency response do you think is most important to practice mentally before you start driving?
2. How does understanding skid recovery connect to what you learned about traction in Module 2.2?
3. Why is the instinctive response (slam brakes, jerk wheel) usually the wrong response in an emergency?

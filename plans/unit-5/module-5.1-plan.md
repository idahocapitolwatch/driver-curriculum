## 3. Pre-Work Plan

**Prior Knowledge Activation:**
- In Module 2.3, you learned about fields of vision and the Smith System. How does reduced visibility affect your ability to apply those techniques?
- What is the most challenging driving condition you have experienced as a passenger? What did the driver do differently?

**Pre-Reading/Viewing:**
- Idaho Driver's Manual, section on Reduced Visibility Driving (~6 pages)
- Guiding questions: When must headlights be turned on in Idaho? When should you switch from high beams to low beams?

**Readiness Self-Check:**
1. Can you name three conditions that reduce visibility?
2. What should you do when facing oncoming headlight glare?
3. Do you know when Idaho law requires headlights to be on?
4. What is the difference between high beams and low beams?

**Key Terms:**
1. Reduced visibility
2. Glare
3. Low beam
4. High beam
5. Over-driving headlights
6. Dawn/dusk
7. Sun visor
8. Daytime running lights
9. Fog lights

| 3 | Night Driving Strategies | 8 min | "Why should you reduce speed at night even if the road is empty?" |

## 5. Activity Plan

### Activity 1: Visibility Condition Risk Chart

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-5.1.1
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. For each of 6 visibility conditions (night, dawn, dusk, fog, sun glare, rain), identify:
   - The specific visibility risk
   - At least two risk reduction procedures
   - The headlight setting appropriate for that condition
2. Compare to the answer key.

**Deliverable:** Completed visibility risk chart

### Activity 2: Night Driving Strategy Scenarios

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-5.1.2, MLO-5.1.3
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Review 6 night driving scenarios.
2. For each, identify: (a) the correct headlight beam setting, (b) appropriate speed adjustment, (c) following distance adjustment, (d) any special procedures needed.
3. Explain your reasoning for each choice.
4. Check your answers.

**Deliverable:** Written scenario analyses with reasoning

### Activity 3: Visibility Safety Discussion

**Type:** Interaction
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Learner
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-5.1.1, MLO-5.1.2
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** Yes (5% of module grade)

**Instructions:**
1. Describe the reduced visibility condition you think is most dangerous in Idaho and explain why.
2. List at least three specific strategies for that condition.
3. Respond to one classmate who chose a different condition — compare risks.

**Deliverable:** Discussion post (minimum 75 words) + 1 response (minimum 30 words)

### Assessment: Module 5.1 Stop and Check Quiz

**Type:** Quiz
**MLO(s) Assessed:** MLO-5.1.1, MLO-5.1.2, MLO-5.1.3
**Weight:** 10% of module grade

**Attempts:** 2 allowed

Quiz covering visibility conditions, night driving strategies, glare management, and Idaho headlight laws.

**Academic Integrity Notice:**
Complete individually. Notes and manual may be referenced. Do not share answers. AI tool use is not permitted.

## 7. Reflection & Synthesis Plan

**Key Takeaways:**
1. Reduced visibility significantly increases crash risk — slower speeds and increased following distances are essential compensations.
2. Glare management requires proactive preparation (clean windshield, visor use) and reactive techniques (look right, reduce speed).
3. Idaho headlight law specifies exactly when lights are required and when to dim — violations endanger everyone.
4. "Over-driving your headlights" (driving faster than your stopping distance within headlight range) is one of the most common and dangerous night driving errors.

**Connection to Next Module:**
Module 5.2 (Driving in Adverse Weather) extends these visibility skills to weather-related challenges — rain, snow, ice, fog, and wind.

**Self-Reflection Prompt:**
1. How does this module connect to what you learned about stopping distance in Module 2.2?
2. What is one change you will make to how you prepare for night driving?
3. Why is it important to keep your headlights and windshield clean?

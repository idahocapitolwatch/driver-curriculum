## 5. Activity Plan

### Activity 1: Three Collisions Analysis

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-5.4.1
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. For each of the three collisions (vehicle, human, internal), describe:
   - What happens in this collision
   - How a seatbelt reduces injury at this stage
   - How an airbag reduces injury at this stage
   - What happens to an unrestrained occupant at this stage
2. Compare to model answers.

**Deliverable:** Completed three-collision analysis chart

### Activity 2: Post-Crash Procedure Sequencing

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-5.4.2, MLO-5.4.3
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Arrange the 10 post-crash steps into correct sequence.
2. Identify which steps involve legal requirements (must do vs. should do).
3. For each step, note the relevant Idaho law.
4. Include when law enforcement must be called vs. when it is optional.
5. Check your answers.

**Deliverable:** Completed sequencing exercise with legal annotations

### Activity 3: Collision Avoidance and Safety Discussion

**Type:** Interaction
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Learner
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-5.4.4
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** Yes (5% of module grade)

**Instructions:**
1. Review the Idaho crash statistics provided.
2. Choose the driver error you think is most preventable through skills learned in this course.
3. Post your analysis: which error, what avoidance strategy from the course addresses it, and data to support your argument.
4. Respond to one classmate — agree, disagree, or add supporting evidence.

**Deliverable:** Discussion post (minimum 100 words) + 1 response (minimum 30 words)

## 7. Reflection & Synthesis Plan

**Key Takeaways:**
1. A crash is really three collisions — vehicle, human, and internal — and seatbelts/airbags protect at every stage.
2. Post-crash procedures follow a strict sequence: stop, protect the scene, call for help, provide aid, exchange information, document, report.
3. Idaho law requires liability insurance — driving without it carries serious penalties and leaves you financially exposed.
4. Most collisions are preventable — the skills learned throughout this course (SIPDE, space management, visibility, speed control) are your best collision avoidance tools.

**Connection to Course Completion:**
This is the final module of DRV101. You have now covered all the knowledge needed to be a safe, responsible driver in Idaho. Your next step is to apply these skills during behind-the-wheel instruction and supervised practice driving.

**Self-Reflection Prompt:**
1. How has your understanding of crash physics changed the way you think about wearing a seatbelt?
2. Looking back across all five units, which concept or skill do you think will have the biggest impact on your driving safety?
3. What is one commitment you will make as a new driver based on everything you have learned in this course?

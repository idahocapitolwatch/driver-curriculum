## 3. Pre-Work Plan

**Prior Knowledge Activation:**
- In Module 2.4, you learned about SIPDE and space management. Now think: how do these skills apply differently when a bicycle is in the lane ahead versus a car?
- Have you ever seen a driver interact unsafely with a pedestrian or cyclist? What happened?

**Pre-Reading/Viewing:**
- Idaho Driver's Manual, section on Sharing the Road (~8 pages)
- Guiding questions: What is Idaho's law on passing cyclists? What should you do when you see an emergency vehicle approaching?

**Readiness Self-Check:**
1. How much space should you give a cyclist when passing?
2. What should you do when an emergency vehicle with lights and sirens approaches?
3. Why are large trucks harder to share the road with?
4. What should you do if a police officer signals you to pull over?

**Key Terms:**
1. Vulnerable road user
2. 3-foot passing rule
3. No-zone (truck blind spots)
4. Pedestrian right of way
5. Emergency vehicle yielding
6. Funeral procession
7. Slow-moving vehicle
8. Traffic stop
9. Implied consent

## 5. Activity Plan

### Activity 1: Road User Responsibility Chart

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-3.1.1
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Complete the chart listing each road user type (pedestrian, cyclist, motorcyclist, truck, emergency vehicle, animal, slow-moving vehicle).
2. For each, write: (a) the primary danger they present, (b) the driver's specific responsibility, (c) the Idaho law that applies.
3. Compare to the completed chart.

**Deliverable:** Completed road user responsibility chart

### Activity 2: Attitude Impact Scenarios

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-3.1.2
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Read 5 scenarios describing driving attitudes/choices (e.g., "A driver is running late and speeds through a school zone," "A driver sees an ambulance but doesn't pull over because traffic is heavy").
2. For each, identify the consequences for (a) the driver, (b) other road users, and (c) the community.
3. Suggest a safer alternative behavior.

**Deliverable:** Written scenario analyses with consequences and alternatives

### Activity 3: Sharing the Road Discussion

**Type:** Interaction
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Learner
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-3.1.1, MLO-3.1.2
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** Yes (5% of module grade)

**Instructions:**
1. Choose one type of vulnerable road user (pedestrian, cyclist, or motorcyclist).
2. Post a discussion entry explaining: why this road user is particularly vulnerable, what drivers should do to protect them, and what Idaho law requires.
3. Respond to one classmate who chose a different road user type.

**Deliverable:** Discussion post (minimum 75 words) + 1 response (minimum 30 words)

## 6. Assessment Plan

### Assessment: Module 3.1 Stop and Check Quizzes

**Type:** Quiz (3 quizzes)
**MLO(s) Assessed:** MLO-3.1.1, MLO-3.1.2, MLO-3.1.3
**Weight:** 10% of module grade
**Duration:** 15 minutes total
**Attempts:** 2 allowed

**Description:**
Quiz 1 covers driving attitudes and consequences. Quiz 2 covers road user responsibilities. Quiz 3 covers traffic stop procedures.

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Road User Responsibilities (MLO-3.1.1) | Correctly explains responsibilities for all road user types | Explains most correctly; minor gap | Explains some; notable omissions | Cannot explain driver responsibilities for most road users |
| Attitude Impact Evaluation (MLO-3.1.2) | Evaluates all scenarios with multiple consequences cited | Evaluates most with adequate consequences | Evaluates some; consequences vague | Cannot evaluate attitude impacts |
| Traffic Stop Procedures (MLO-3.1.3) | Lists all procedures in correct sequence with no omissions | Lists most in correct sequence; minor omission | Lists some steps; sequence errors | Cannot list traffic stop procedures |

**Academic Integrity Notice:**
Complete individually. Notes and manual may be referenced. Do not share answers. AI tool use is not permitted.

## 7. Reflection & Synthesis Plan

**Key Takeaways:**
1. Every road user — pedestrian, cyclist, truck driver, or animal — requires specific awareness and driving adjustments from you.
2. Your driving attitude directly affects the safety of everyone in the Highway Transportation System — one careless choice can have irreversible consequences.
3. During a traffic stop, following the correct procedures (pull over, engine off, hands visible, cooperate) ensures safety for you and the officer.

**Connection to Next Module:**
Module 3.2 (Turning and Parking) teaches the specific maneuvers you need to navigate around other road users — turns, turnabouts, and parking in shared spaces.

**Self-Reflection Prompt:**
1. Which road user type do you think drivers overlook most often? Why?
2. How does your attitude toward other road users affect your driving decisions?
3. Before this module, did you know all the steps for a proper traffic stop? Which step was new to you?

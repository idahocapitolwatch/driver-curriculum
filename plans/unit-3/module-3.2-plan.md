## 3. Pre-Work Plan

**Prior Knowledge Activation:**
- In Module 1.2, you learned about steering techniques and backing. How will those skills apply when performing precision turns and parking maneuvers?
- Have you ever watched someone parallel park? What steps did they take?

**Pre-Reading/Viewing:**
- Idaho Driver's Manual, sections on Turns, Turnabouts, and Parking (~10 pages)
- Guiding questions: What is the difference between a 2-point and 3-point turn? Which direction do you turn your wheels when parked downhill with a curb?

**Readiness Self-Check:**
1. Can you describe hand-over-hand steering?
2. Do you know what a 3-point turn is?
3. What direction should you turn wheels when parked uphill with a curb?
4. Is it legal to make a U-turn at any intersection?

**Key Terms:**
1. Precision turn
2. 90-degree corner
3. U-turn
4. 2-point turn
5. 3-point turn
6. Parallel parking
7. Perpendicular parking
8. Angled parking
9. Hill parking
10. Restricted parking zone

## 5. Activity Plan

### Activity 1: Turn Procedure Sequencing

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-3.2.1
**Estimated Time:** 10 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Arrange the steps for a left precision turn from a stopped position in correct order.
2. Repeat for a right precision turn from a moving position.
3. Check your answers against the correct sequence.

**Deliverable:** Two completed sequencing exercises

### Activity 2: Turnabout Selection Activity

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-3.2.2
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Review 6 scenarios describing different road situations (narrow street, wide residential road, busy intersection, etc.).
2. For each, select the safest turnabout type and explain why.
3. Write the step-by-step procedure for the turnabout you selected.
4. Compare to model answers.

**Deliverable:** Turnabout selections with explanations and procedures

### Activity 3: Parking Scenario Practice

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-3.2.3
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Review 8 parking scenario images (angled lot, parallel curb, perpendicular lot, uphill with curb, uphill without curb, downhill with curb, downhill without curb, restricted zone).
2. Identify the parking type for each and describe the correct procedure.
3. For hill parking, draw or describe the wheel direction.
4. Check your answers.

**Deliverable:** Completed parking scenario worksheet

## 6. Assessment Plan

### Assessment: Module 3.2 Stop and Check Quizzes

**Type:** Quiz (3 quizzes)
**MLO(s) Assessed:** MLO-3.2.1, MLO-3.2.2, MLO-3.2.3
**Weight:** 10% of module grade
**Duration:** 20 minutes total
**Attempts:** 2 allowed

**Description:**
Quiz 1 covers precision turns and corners. Quiz 2 covers turnabout types and selection. Quiz 3 covers parking types and hill parking.

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Precision Turns (MLO-3.2.1) | Describes all turn procedures with all critical steps and correct sequence | Describes most steps; minor omission | Describes some steps; notable gaps | Cannot describe turn procedures |
| Turnabout Selection (MLO-3.2.2) | Correctly selects and explains all turnabout types with accurate procedures | Selects correctly for most; minor procedure error | Selects correctly for some; procedure gaps | Cannot select appropriate turnabouts |
| Parking Procedures (MLO-3.2.3) | Identifies all parking types and procedures at 90%+ accuracy including hill parking | Identifies most at 80–89%; hill parking mostly correct | Identifies some at 60–79%; hill parking errors | Cannot identify parking types or procedures |

**Academic Integrity Notice:**
Complete individually. Notes and manual may be referenced. Do not share answers. AI tool use is not permitted.

## 7. Reflection & Synthesis Plan

**Key Takeaways:**
1. Precision turns require a systematic approach: signal, position, slow, look, turn, accelerate.
2. Choosing the right turnabout depends on the road environment — always pick the safest option for the situation.
3. Parking skills combine reference points, steering techniques, and spatial awareness — parallel parking is the most complex but follows predictable steps.
4. Hill parking wheel positioning is a safety essential — the goal is always to prevent the vehicle from rolling into traffic.

**Connection to Next Module:**
Module 3.3 (Lane Changes and Passing) teaches you how to safely change lanes and pass other vehicles — skills that require the same space management and mirror use you practiced here.

**Self-Reflection Prompt:**
1. Which parking maneuver do you think you need to practice most? Why?
2. How does the choice of turnabout affect the safety of other road users around you?
3. Can you explain the hill parking wheel rule in your own words so a friend would understand it?

- Hill parking diagrams use arrows with text labels, not color alone.

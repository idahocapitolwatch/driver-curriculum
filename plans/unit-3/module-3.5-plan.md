## 3. Pre-Work Plan

**Prior Knowledge Activation:**
- How is driving in a busy city different from the rural driving you studied in Module 3.4?
- Have you ever been on a freeway as a passenger? What was different about it compared to regular roads?

**Pre-Reading/Viewing:**
- Idaho Driver's Manual, sections on Urban and Freeway Driving (~10 pages)
- Guiding questions: What are the advantages of freeways? How do you enter a freeway safely?

**Readiness Self-Check:**
1. What does "limited access highway" mean?
2. How do you merge onto a freeway?
3. What are the main hazards in city driving?
4. What should you do if you miss your freeway exit?

**Key Terms:**
1. Urban driving
2. Limited access highway (freeway)
3. Acceleration lane (on-ramp)
4. Deceleration lane (off-ramp)
5. Interchange
6. Cloverleaf
7. Diamond interchange
8. Congestion
9. One-way street
10. Speed differential

## 5. Activity Plan

### Activity 1: Urban vs. Rural Hazard Comparison

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-3.5.1
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Complete a comparison chart listing hazards unique to urban driving vs. rural driving (from Module 3.4).
2. For each urban hazard, describe the driving adjustment required.
3. Compare to the answer key.

**Deliverable:** Completed urban vs. rural hazard comparison chart

### Activity 2: Freeway Maneuver Sequencing

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-3.5.2
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Arrange the steps for entering a freeway into correct order.
2. Arrange the steps for exiting a freeway into correct order.
3. For each sequence, identify the most critical safety step and explain why.
4. Check your answers.

**Deliverable:** Two completed sequences with critical step identification

### Activity 3: Driving Environment Discussion

**Type:** Interaction
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Learner
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-3.5.1, MLO-3.5.3
**Estimated Time:** 15 minutes
**Graded:** Yes (5% of module grade)

**Instructions:**
1. Choose either urban or freeway driving.
2. Post a discussion entry describing the three hazards you think are most dangerous and the strategies to manage them.
3. Respond to one classmate who chose the other driving environment.

**Deliverable:** Discussion post (minimum 75 words) + 1 response (minimum 30 words)

## 6. Assessment Plan

### Assessment: Module 3.5 Stop and Check Quizzes

**Type:** Quiz (2 quizzes)
**MLO(s) Assessed:** MLO-3.5.1, MLO-3.5.2, MLO-3.5.3
**Weight:** 10% of module grade
**Duration:** 15 minutes total
**Attempts:** 2 allowed

**Description:**
Quiz 1 covers urban driving characteristics and hazards. Quiz 2 covers freeway procedures and interchange identification.

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Urban Hazards (MLO-3.5.1) | Identifies 6+ hazards with correct adjustments | Identifies 4–5 with mostly correct adjustments | Identifies 2–3; adjustments incomplete | Cannot identify urban-specific hazards |
| Freeway Procedures (MLO-3.5.2) | Describes enter/exit/lane change procedures with all steps | Describes most procedures; minor omission | Describes some; critical steps missing | Cannot describe freeway procedures |
| Interchange Identification (MLO-3.5.3) | Identifies all interchange types with correct navigation at 90%+ | Identifies most at 80–89% | Identifies some at 60–79% | Cannot identify interchange types |

**Academic Integrity Notice:**
Complete individually. Notes and manual may be referenced. Do not share answers. AI tool use is not permitted.

## 7. Reflection & Synthesis Plan

**Key Takeaways:**
1. Urban driving presents unique challenges: congestion, pedestrians, frequent intersections, and complex signage.
2. Freeways offer advantages (high speed, controlled access) but demand faster decision-making and greater following distances.
3. Merging safely requires matching traffic speed before entering the travel lane — never stop on an acceleration ramp unless absolutely necessary.
4. If you miss an exit, proceed to the next one — never back up or stop on a freeway.

**Connection to Next Module:**
Unit 4 begins with Module 4.1 (Distracted Driving), which addresses one of the greatest risks across ALL driving environments — distraction.

**Self-Reflection Prompt:**
1. Which do you think is more challenging: urban driving or freeway driving? Why?
2. How do congestion and time pressure affect your driving attitude and risk level?
3. What will you do differently the first time you merge onto a freeway as a driver?

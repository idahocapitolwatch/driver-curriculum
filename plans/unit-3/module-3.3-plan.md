## 3. Pre-Work Plan

**Prior Knowledge Activation:**
- In Module 2.4, you learned about SIPDE and gap selection. How do those skills apply to changing lanes on a highway?
- What is the biggest risk when passing another vehicle on a two-lane road?

**Pre-Reading/Viewing:**
- Idaho Driver's Manual, sections on Lane Changes and Passing (~8 pages)
- Guiding questions: When is it illegal to pass? What is a "head check" and why is it important?

**Readiness Self-Check:**
1. What is a blind spot?
2. Do you know when you are not allowed to pass another vehicle?
3. What signal should you use before changing lanes?
4. What does a solid yellow center line mean for passing?

**Key Terms:**
1. Lane change
2. Blind spot (blind area)
3. Head check (shoulder check)
4. Gap selection
5. No-passing zone
6. Passing
7. Lane return
8. Solid yellow line
9. Broken yellow line
10. Speed differential

## 5. Activity Plan

### Activity 1: Lane Change Procedure Practice

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-3.3.1, MLO-3.3.3
**Estimated Time:** 10 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Arrange the 7 lane change steps into correct order.
2. For each step, identify which mirror(s) or check(s) are used.
3. Check your answers against the model.

**Deliverable:** Completed lane change sequence with mirror/check identification

### Activity 2: Passing Decision Scenarios

**Type:** Application
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-3.3.2
**Estimated Time:** 20 minutes
**Graded:** Yes (5% of module grade)

**Instructions:**
1. Review 8 two-lane road scenarios with diagrams.
2. For each, determine: (a) Is passing legal? (b) Is passing safe? (c) If yes to both, describe the passing procedure. If no to either, explain why not.
3. Submit your analysis.

**Deliverable:** Written pass/no-pass decisions with explanations for all 8 scenarios

### Activity 3: Blind Spot Identification Exercise

**Type:** Practice
**Interaction Mode:** Learner-Content
**MLO(s) Addressed:** MLO-3.3.3
**Estimated Time:** 10 minutes
**Graded:** No (formative)

**Instructions:**
1. Review overhead diagrams for a passenger car, SUV, and semi-truck.
2. Shade or identify all blind spot areas on each diagram.
3. For each vehicle type, describe how you would check the blind spot before a lane change.
4. Check your answers.

**Deliverable:** Completed blind spot diagrams with check procedures

## 6. Assessment Plan

### Assessment: Module 3.3 Stop and Check Quizzes

**Type:** Quiz (2 quizzes)
**MLO(s) Assessed:** MLO-3.3.1, MLO-3.3.2, MLO-3.3.3
**Weight:** 10% of module grade
**Duration:** 15 minutes total
**Attempts:** 2 allowed

**Description:**
Quiz 1 covers lane change procedures and blind spots. Quiz 2 covers passing requirements and scenarios.

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Lane Change Procedure (MLO-3.3.1) | Describes all steps in correct order with no omissions | Describes most steps; minor sequence issue | Describes some steps; notable gaps | Cannot describe lane change procedure |
| Passing Evaluation (MLO-3.3.2) | Correctly evaluates 90%+ of passing scenarios for legality and safety | Correctly evaluates 80–89% | Correctly evaluates 60–79% | Correctly evaluates fewer than 60% |

**Academic Integrity Notice:**
Complete individually. Notes and manual may be referenced. Do not share answers. AI tool use is not permitted.

## 7. Reflection & Synthesis Plan

**Key Takeaways:**
1. A safe lane change follows a strict sequence: mirror, signal, head check, gap, move, cancel — every step matters.
2. Passing on two-lane roads is one of the most dangerous maneuvers — it must be both legal AND safe.
3. Blind spots exist on every vehicle — the head check (shoulder check) is non-negotiable before any lane change.
4. When being passed, your job is to make it easier and safer for the passing driver.

**Connection to Next Module:**
Module 3.4 (Rural Driving) will place lane changes and passing skills in the context of rural roads — where higher speeds and limited passing opportunities make these skills critical.

**Self-Reflection Prompt:**
1. Why do you think failing to check blind spots is one of the top causes of lane-change crashes?
2. What conditions would make you decide NOT to pass, even when it is legal?
3. How does this module connect to what you learned about space management in Module 2.4?
